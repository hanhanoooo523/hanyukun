Biodesign Project Planning Document

Symbio-Shell — a speculative living interface for handheld devices, framed as critical / biodesign fiction. This plan covers narrative, visualization, and responsible communication only.

Scope: no working prototype, no wet-lab work, no claims of clinical performance. Success is measured clarity of intent, visual storytelling, and ethics-aware framing.

Document type: course-style biodesign planning template, applied to Symbio-Shell.
Companion: concept narrative and imagery live in Symbio-Shell-共生壳-概念页.html (Chinese long-form).

1 Project identity & scope

FieldContent
TitleSymbio-Shell（共生壳）
One-linerA metabolically legible phone enclosure concept that couples physiological signals, waste heat, and microbial metaphor to make digital anxiety and dependency felt—not merely measured.
Biodesign lensUses motifs from synthetic biology, microfluidics, hydrogels, and bio-interfaces as design vocabulary, not as validated engineering specs.
Declared boundarySpeculative biodesign for portfolio, exhibition, or classroom critique. All “sensing” and “metabolism” are narrative devices unless future work opens a separate prototype track.

2 Problem framing & need statement

Observed condition: Mobile devices extend perception yet dull embodied intuition about stress, thermal load, and material waste. Accessories feel disposable and psychically “dead.”

Design question: How might a biotic layer re-introduce embodiment, accountability, and ritual around everyday compute—without pretending to be a medical device?

Need statement (for critique):

People who feel “numb” to their digital habits need a fictionally coherent object language—visual, tactile, and procedural—that makes interdependence with technology emotionally and narratively legible, so we can argue about care, limits, and end-of-use in public.

Important: Cortisol, stomata-like valves, and MFC arrays are story elements. This document does not claim biomarker accuracy or regulatory readiness.

4 Design brief & biological concept strategy

Translate the fiction into design criteria you can grade in studio (coherence, ethics, communication), not manufacturing KPIs.

CriterionIntent
Layered anatomyFour functional strata (epidermis → microfluidics → stomata → bio-electrochemical base) read as one diagram; labels match the concept page glossary.
Feedback loopsStress / heat / neglect each map to distinct sensory channels (color, texture metaphor, “breath,” desiccation)—described, not instrumented.
Ritual closure“Recovery” scenes (light, moisture, contact) carry ethical payload: stepping outside, maintenance-as-relation, not punishment-as-default.
End-of-life storyComposting / return-to-soil narrative distinguishes the project from throwaway accessories.

Concept-stage “research” (desk only)

  • Microfluidic colorimetry — cite reviews or popular science to show the trope is in conversation with real labs; mark speculative amplification.
  • Hydrogel mechanics — enough to justify a plausible texture story; no formulation.
  • Thermo-responsive materials — analogy tier only.

5 Milestones & work plan

Two rhythms: a full concept arc (~4–6 months light part-time) or a critique sprint (~6–8 weeks).

PhaseOutcome
P0 AlignLock scope, success rubric, and disclaimer language (“speculative biodesign”).
P1 Systems storyOne system diagram + assumption table; annotate “inferred / fictional strength.”
P2 Narrative & visualsHero imagery, anatomical plate, storyboards (high load / neglect / recovery).
P3 SynthesisDeck, talk track, mock Q&A; export web or PDF “freeze” for submission.

Sprint checklist (example)

  1. Weeks 1–2: system sketch + stakeholder paragraph.
  2. Weeks 3–4: narrative lock + primary visual.
  3. Weeks 5–6: deepen storyboard or 30–60s motion script.
  4. Week 7: rehearsal + ethics one-pager.
  5. Week 8: final layout and asset archive.

6 Ethics, biosafety & responsible hype

  • No diagnostic or therapeutic claims; no human-subjects framing in this phase.
  • Avoid implying shipped consumer hardware; prefer “concept,” “proposal,” or “design fiction.”
  • Where metaphors are harsh (“sensory coercion”), prepare a softer academic wording for mixed audiences.
  • If imagery suggests bodily fluids, caption as symbolic / non-biohazard staging.
  • Future wet work requires a separate risk assessment and institutional process—out of scope here.

7 Risks & mitigations

RiskMitigation
Misread as near-market productBanner disclaimer on every public surface; diagram footnotes for fictional elements.
“Anti-user” interpretationPair discomfort mechanics with care rhetoric; emphasize debate, not deployment.
Fact-check pushbackShort bibliography + explicit limits; poetic/ethical intent may outweigh engineering fidelity in critique.
Time shortfallCut literature extras; keep one storyline + one visual system.

8 Deliverables & success criteria

Checklist for instructors or reviewers (concept track):

  • This planning doc + bilingual counterpart if required.
  • System diagram + explicit assumption / speculation key.
  • Primary visual suite (exploded anatomy, mechanism stills, stomata macro series).
  • Presentation medium: HTML concept page, slide deck, and/or short film treatment.
  • One-page ethics & “what we are not claiming.”
  • Optional: 1–3 page annotated bibliography (review / pop-sci level).

Success (minimum): a visitor can summarize problem → biological metaphor → feedback ethics → circularity story in under two minutes using your materials alone.
